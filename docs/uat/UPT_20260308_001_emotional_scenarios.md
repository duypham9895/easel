## Scenario A — The Argument

**Context:** Day 18 of simulation. Linh is in the luteal phase (day ~18 of her 28-day cycle = post-ovulatory, PMS territory). Minh comes home late and forgets they had plans. Linh is more upset than usual.

### Linh (Moon) — First Person

I already knew today was going to be hard. The app greeted me this morning with something like "Tu tu thoi. Ban khong can phai lam het moi thu" — *Slow down. You don't have to do everything.* The phase chip says "Hoang the - Lang diu" (Luteal - Wind Down). I did my check-in: mood 2 (Meh), symptoms: Mood swings, Fatigue. The AI insight validated me — said something about how it's normal to feel more sensitive right now.

But here's the thing: by the time Minh forgot our plans and walked in at 9pm, I wasn't thinking about the app. I was angry. I opened the app after the fight to see if it could help me explain what happened. The "About this phase" card says: "Ban co the nhay cam hoac de cau hon. Trieu chung tien kinh nguyet co the xuat hien." That's... accurate. But it's explaining ME to me. I already know why I'm upset. What I needed was for Minh to have already known.

I thought about sending him a Whisper. The luteal options are: "Mang do an vat" (Bring snacks), "Cho em khong gian" (Give me space), "Om em" (Cuddle me), "Loi diu dang" (Kind words). After a fight? None of these fit. "Give me space" is the closest but it sounds passive. I could type a custom whisper, but at that point I'd rather just text him. The Whisper feature feels designed for soft moments, not post-conflict repair.

**Emotional verdict:** The app understood my body but couldn't help me navigate the fight. It explained the fight after the fact — it didn't prevent it.

### Minh (Sun) — First Person

I opened the app around lunchtime today. The status card showed "Hoang the" (Luteal), day 18, and the countdown badge said 11 days until her period. The guide section had "Tam trang Moon luc nay" showing "Co ay co the nhay cam hoac de cau hon. Trieu chung tien kinh nguyet co the xuat hien." — She may be more sensitive or irritable. PMS symptoms can emerge.

The AI advice card ("Cach the hien") told me something specific like "Be patient, listen without judgment." But I read that at lunch and forgot about it by 7pm when work ran late. There was no reminder, no push notification saying "Hey, Moon is in luteal — be extra thoughtful tonight." I only check the app when I remember to, and today I forgot.

After the fight, I opened the app again. The advice was still there, staring at me. I felt guilty. The app had literally told me to "tranh tranh cai" (avoid arguments), and I'd walked right into one.

**Emotional verdict:** The information was there but it didn't reach me when it mattered. By the time I read it again, it was too late.

### Critical Questions

**Did the app help Minh understand this moment?**
Partially. The information was available (luteal = more sensitive, avoid arguments), but delivery failed. Minh read it at lunch and forgot. No push notification reinforced it at a critical moment. The app is a pull experience for Sun — he must actively open it. For relationship-critical moments like luteal phase evenings, the app needs to push.

**Did Linh feel supported?**
By the app, mildly — the morning greeting and check-in insight validated her feelings. By Minh through the app, no. The app didn't bridge the gap between knowing and acting.

**Did the app help them fight LESS, or just explain the fight?**
It explained the fight. The luteal advice ("avoid arguments," "be patient") was accurate but wasn't delivered at the right time. The app currently has no mechanism to intervene proactively when a conflict-prone phase intersects with real life.

**GAP IDENTIFIED:**
- **P0 — Missing luteal/PMS proactive notifications for Sun.** A push notification like "Moon dang trong giai doan nhay cam — hay nhe nhang toi nay" (Moon is in a sensitive phase — be gentle tonight) sent at 5-6pm could prevent arguments.
- **P1 — No post-conflict Whisper options.** The luteal whisper set lacks emotional repair signals like "I'm sorry I snapped" or "I need you to understand."
- **P2 — No "why I'm feeling this" shareable card.** Linh wants to show Minh the phase explanation but the Moon dashboard is personal — there's no "share this with Sun" action.

## Scenario C — The Health Concern

**Context:** Linh's cycle is 5 days late. She's worried.

### Linh (Moon) — First Person

My period was supposed to start 5 days ago. I open the app and... nothing has changed. The Phase Wheel still shows me in the same position it would based on my average 28-day cycle. The `cycleCalculator.ts` uses modular arithmetic: `(diffDays % avgCycleLength) + 1`. So if I'm on day 33 of a 28-day cycle, the calculator wraps around and shows me at day 5 — as if I've already started a new cycle. The app thinks I'm in my menstrual phase of a new cycle.

This is deeply unsettling. The greeting might say something about rest and restore. The phase chip shows "Kinh nguyet - Nghi ngoi & Phuc hoi." But I haven't started bleeding. The app is wrong, and it doesn't know it's wrong because I have no way to confirm or deny that my period has started.

I look for a way to tell the app "my period hasn't started yet." The Daily Check-in lets me log mood and symptoms, but there's no "period started/not started" toggle. The Settings screen lets me change `lastPeriodStartDate`, but manually editing a date field to signal "I'm late" feels wrong — clinical, not supportive.

I'm scared. Am I pregnant? Is something wrong? The app has no late-period flow, no reassurance, no "here's what a late period might mean" content. It just... pretends nothing happened and wraps around to a phantom Day 5.

### Minh (Sun) — First Person

I have no idea she's late. My dashboard pulls her cycle settings via `partnerCycleSettings` and runs the same `cycleCalculator` — so it also wraps around and shows "Kinh nguyet." I might text her "hey, hope you're resting" based on the app, not knowing she's actually anxious about being late. The app gives me no signal that something unexpected is happening.

### Critical Questions

**How does the app respond?**
It doesn't. The modular cycle calculation wraps past the expected period start date without any awareness of whether the period actually began. There is no late-period detection, no confirmation flow, and no deviation handling.

**Does it feel supportive or clinical?**
Neither — it feels oblivious. The app is cheerfully telling Linh about her menstrual phase self-care while she's staring at a negative pregnancy test. This is a trust-breaking moment.

**Does it help them navigate TOGETHER or isolate Linh?**
It isolates Linh completely. Minh sees the same incorrect phase data and has no reason to check in about a late period. The app actively misleads him.

**GAP IDENTIFIED:**
- **P0 — CRITICAL: No period confirmation flow.** The app must ask "Has your period started?" near the expected date instead of auto-advancing. This is the single most dangerous UX gap in the app.
- **P0 — No late-period detection.** If `dayInCycle > avgCycleLength + 2`, the app should surface a "Your period seems late" notification with supportive content (not medical advice, but reassurance and "talk to your doctor if concerned").
- **P1 — No late-period signal to Sun.** If Moon's period is significantly late, Sun should get a gentle heads-up: "Moon's cycle is a bit different this month — be extra supportive."
- **P2 — Cycle calculator wraps unsafely.** The modulo wrapping in `getCurrentDayInCycle` should clamp at `avgCycleLength + N` instead of wrapping, so the dashboard shows "Day 33 of expected 28-day cycle" rather than pretending it's Day 5.

## Scenario G — The SOS Moment

**Context:** Linh has severe cramps and needs Minh to bring something from the store.

### Linh (Moon) — First Person

I'm on the couch with cramps. I open the app and find the SOS button... wait, where is it? The SOS Sheet is triggered from the dashboard, but looking at the MoonDashboard code, I see the main CTA button is "Thi tham voi Sun" (Whisper to Sun), not SOS. The SOS option is in the SOSSheet component, but where is it triggered from the dashboard?

Looking at the tab layout, the SOS button appears to be in the bottom tab bar (the `_layout.tsx` file). So I need to find it there. The four SOS options appear: "Them do ngot" (Sweet Tooth), "Can om" (Need a Hug), "Dau bung kinh" (Cramps Alert), "Can yen tinh" (Quiet Time).

I tap "Dau bung kinh" (Cramps Alert). The haptic fires. The sheet closes. But now — does Minh know I need ibuprofen and a heating pad from the store? The SOS options are categorical signals, not specific requests. "Cramps Alert" tells him I'm in pain but not what to bring. I'd have to follow up with a text saying "can you get ibuprofen?"

### Minh (Sun) — First Person

My phone buzzes — push notification. I open the app. A red card dominates the screen: "TIN HIEU SOS" (SOS Signal), "Co ay can: Dau bung kinh" (She needs: Cramps Alert). The AI tip underneath says something like "Grab a heating pad and ibuprofen — menstrual cramps peak in the first 48 hours." That's actually helpful and specific.

But — is this faster than a phone call? She had to unlock her phone, open the app, find the SOS button, select "Cramps Alert," wait for the signal to reach me via Realtime/push. A phone call would be: press call, say "bring ibuprofen." 30 seconds vs. maybe 15 seconds.

The SOS card stays for 5 minutes (the `_sosTimer` in the store). That's enough time. I tap "Got it" and head to the store.

### Critical Questions

**How fast does it reach Minh?**
Depends on the delivery path. Realtime (foreground): near-instant. Push notification (background): 1-10 seconds depending on APNs. Total time from Linh's tap to Minh reading: 5-15 seconds. Acceptable but not faster than calling.

**Does he know exactly what to do?**
The AI tip helps significantly — it gives concrete suggestions (heating pad, ibuprofen) that the raw SOS signal doesn't. Without the AI tip, "Cramps Alert" is vague. The AI tip is the difference between "she's in pain" and "here's what to do about it."

**In a real emergency, would they use the app or just call?**
They'd call for true emergencies. The SOS feature serves the "I need something but don't want to have a whole conversation" use case — not actual medical emergencies. The 4-option grid is designed for common comfort needs, not urgent communication. This positioning is appropriate, but the word "SOS" might overpromise urgency.

**GAP IDENTIFIED:**
- **P1 — SOS lacks specific request detail.** A "Add a note" field (like WhisperSheet's custom input) would let Linh specify "ibuprofen + heating pad" alongside the categorical signal.
- **P2 — SOS naming may overpromise.** "SOS" implies emergency-level urgency. The actual options (sweet tooth, hug, cramps, quiet time) are comfort needs. Consider renaming to "Signal" or keeping SOS but adding a disclaimer.

She's right — I haven't opened it in 4 days. Nothing pushed me to open it. The phase didn't change (I was in luteal the whole time), no SOS came in, no whisper came in... wait, she says she sent a whisper? I check my notifications — I might have swiped it away without reading, or the push didn't reach me, or I was in a meeting.

**Does Linh have visibility into his engagement?**
None. There is no "last seen" indicator, no read receipt on whispers, no engagement dashboard. Linh has zero way to know if Minh is using the app.

Currently, no — because there's no surveillance data. But this creates a DIFFERENT tension: the "am I doing this alone?" feeling. **GAP IDENTIFIED:**
- **P0 — Whispers have no delivery/read confirmation.** Linh sends a whisper and sees "He will know what to do" — but if he never opens the app, he doesn't. At minimum, a "Delivered" / "Seen" status on the whisper history would close this loop.

- **P1 — Whisper auto-clear is too aggressive.** 5 minutes is not enough. If Minh opens the app 2 hours after a whisper, it's already gone. Whispers should persist until dismissed OR until a reasonable timeout (e.g., 24 hours).
- **P2 — No "engagement indicator" for Moon.** A subtle "Sun last checked in: 2 days ago" on Moon's dashboard would set expectations without creating surveillance. Must be opt-in and carefully designed to avoid relationship damage.

## Scenario I — The New Cycle

**Context:** New menstrual cycle begins. Linh logs her period start.

### Linh (Moon) — First Person

My period started today. I go to Settings to update my `lastPeriodStartDate`. Wait — that's the flow? I change a date in Settings? There's no "Period Started" button, no prominent CTA, no ritual moment. I adjust the date picker in the cycle settings section, changing the "Last period start date" field to today.

The dashboard recalculates immediately — the Phase Wheel jumps to Day 1, the phase chip changes to "Kinh nguyet - Nghi ngoi & Phuc hoi" (Menstrual - Rest & Restore), the gradient shifts to red-pink. The greeting changes to something like "Nghi ngoi cung la lam viec hieu qua. Hay nhe nhang voi ban than hom nay" (Rest is productive too. Be gentle with yourself today).

The transition is instant but unceremonious. There's no acknowledgment like "New cycle started!" or "Welcome to Day 1" — it's just a date picker change. For something as significant as the start of a new cycle, this feels anticlimactic. The app treats it like updating a form field, not like a meaningful body event.

The daily check-in appears fresh (since it's a new day), and the phase-specific whisper options switch to menstrual: "Can om" (Need a hug), "Mang hoi am" (Bring warmth), "Mang so-co-la" (Bring chocolate), "Thoi gian yen tinh" (Quiet time). These feel right for today.

### Minh (Sun) — First Person

Does my app update? Let me check. Linh changed her `cycleSettings.lastPeriodStartDate` via `updateCycleSettings`, which calls `upsertCycleSettings` to Supabase. But my app loaded her cycle settings during `bootstrapSession` or `linkToPartner` and stored them in `partnerCycleSettings`. There's no Realtime subscription on the `cycle_settings` table for Sun — the `useCoupleLinkedListener` hook only watches the `couples` table for status changes, not cycle updates.

So my dashboard still shows whatever phase it was showing before until I close and reopen the app (triggering `bootstrapSession`). If I opened the app yesterday and it showed "Hoang the Day 28," today it might calculate Day 1 from the OLD lastPeriodStartDate and show me a different (possibly wrong) day. If Linh updated her period start date to today, my app won't reflect that until I restart.

I don't get a notification saying "Moon's period started." The `notify-cycle` Edge Function runs on a cron schedule and sends period-approaching notifications, but there's no "period has started" push event triggered by the date change.

### Critical Questions

**Does the app acknowledge the transition?**
Barely. The dashboard recalculates the phase and greeting, but there's no transitional moment — no "New cycle!" banner, no celebration/acknowledgment of cycle tracking continuity. The period start is a date picker edit, not a meaningful interaction.

**Does Minh get notified?**
No. There is no Realtime subscription on `cycle_settings` for Sun, and no push notification triggered by period start. Minh remains on stale data until he restarts the app.

**Does the greeting/insight change?**
Yes — the AI greeting and phase description update immediately on Moon's dashboard because they're derived from the new `dayInCycle` calculation. But this is a side effect of recalculation, not an intentional transition.

**GAP IDENTIFIED:**
- **P0 — No "Period Started" CTA.** The most important recurring user action (logging period start) is buried in Settings as a date picker. This needs a prominent, emotionally designed flow — either a button on the dashboard or a prompted action near the expected date.
- **P0 — Sun doesn't get real-time cycle updates.** When Moon updates her period start date, Sun's `partnerCycleSettings` remains stale until app restart. A Realtime subscription on `cycle_settings` changes (filtered to partner's user_id) is needed.
- **P1 — No "period started" notification to Sun.** A push notification like "Chu ky moi cua Moon da bat dau — hay o ben co ay" (Moon's new cycle has started — be there for her) would be one of the app's most valuable notifications.
